shift(duration) 

void shift( D )( D duration ) nothrow pure

Shifts the begin of this interval forward or backwards in time by the given duration (a positive duration shifts the interval forward; a negative duration shifts it backward). Effectively, it does $(D begin += duration).

Params

duration D

The duration to shift the interval by.

Example

-------------------- auto interval1 = PosInfInterval!Date(Date(1996, 1, 2)); auto interval2 = PosInfInterval!Date(Date(1996, 1, 2));

interval1.shift(dur!"days"(50)); assert(interval1 == PosInfInterval!Date(Date(1996, 2, 21)));

interval2.shift(dur!"days"(-50)); assert(interval2 == PosInfInterval!Date(Date(1995, 11, 13))); --------------------

shift(years, months, allowOverflow)

Shifts the begin of this interval forward or backwards in time by the given number of years and/or months (a positive number of years and months shifts the interval forward; a negative number shifts it backward). It adds the years the given years and months to begin . It effectively calls add!"years"() and then add!"months"() on begin with the given number of years and months.

Params

years T

The number of years to shift the interval by.

months T

The number of months to shift the interval by.

allowOverflow AllowDayOverflow

Whether the days should be allowed to overflow on begin , causing its month to increment.

Throws

$(REF DateTimeException,std,datetime,date) if this interval is empty or if the resulting interval would be invalid.

Example

-------------------- auto interval1 = PosInfInterval!Date(Date(1996, 1, 2)); auto interval2 = PosInfInterval!Date(Date(1996, 1, 2));

interval1.shift(dur!"days"(50)); assert(interval1 == PosInfInterval!Date(Date(1996, 2, 21)));

interval2.shift(dur!"days"(-50)); assert(interval2 == PosInfInterval!Date(Date(1995, 11, 13))); --------------------
